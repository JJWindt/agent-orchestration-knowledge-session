**Guidelines up-sell arguments**

**Goal**  
The goal of an upsell collection is to offer better options starting from the entry level, structured from low end to best. For the new MVP going live in the green zone, we want to deliver proof-of-concept upsell content so the design can be tested. Within scope, we only look at the green zone for specific clusters, with the goal of verifying whether we can generate this content using the information currently available in Selma.

**Length**

* Maximum of 100 characters including spaces

**Messaging principles**

* Describe the customer’s needs and clearly indicate the concession they make compared to a better model.  
* Only one upsell option is visible at any time: always the “next best” product compared to the current one, where we stay within one consideration.  
* For each next, better product: describe the improved value compared to the previous model/value. We never mention the previous option explicitly, we only highlight the benefit of the upsell model itself.  
* Write the *best* options persuasively (use “best of the best” or “never worry again” angles where possible).  
* Translate brand-specific terms into a clear, recognizable customer need (e.g. “less ironing and cleaner laundry” instead of “ProSteam” and “ÖKOMix”).

**Writing tips**

* Convince the customer in one short, personal sentence why this upsell will make them happier with their purchase.  
* Use “you get …” where possible and describe recognizable situations so the customer feels they really need this feature.  
* Write in short, active sentences at B1 level, using simple words and putting the customer (“you”) at the center.

**Examples**

| **\-** | You get extra space for towels and bedding, so you wash more in one go. | You get enough space to wash big items like duvets and blankets in one load. |
